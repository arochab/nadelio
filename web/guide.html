± Nadelio The shift, and what we measure
01

Your buyers stopped scrolling. They started asking.

When a customer wants "the best tool for X", more of them now ask an AI assistant instead of scanning ten blue links. The assistant answers with a short list of names. If yours is not on it, you were never in the room, and you will never see the click you lost.

This is the quiet part of the shift. Search used to show everyone and let the buyer choose. AI answers choose first, then show a few. Being on page one of Google and being named by an assistant are now two different races, and a brand can be winning one while losing the other.

Most companies cannot see this happening. They watch their Google rank, feel safe, and never notice that the assistant recommends a competitor for the exact question their best customers ask. Nadelio makes both races visible on one screen.

AI answers

We put the same questions to an AI assistant and record which brands it recommends, in what order, and whether yours is the main answer or an afterthought.

One brand name is all we need to start. Nadelio identifies your competitive set, writes the questions a real buyer would ask, and measures both territories. No setup, no account, about ten seconds.

± Nadelio Why the interval is the whole point
02

A single number is a coin flip wearing a suit.

Ask an assistant the same question ten times and you will not get the same answer ten times. The models are built to vary. So a tool that asks once and prints a score is reporting a coin flip as if it were a fact.

This is where the whole category quietly fails. Every dashboard hands you a confident number. Almost none of them tell you how much that number would move if they measured again. In a controlled test, a reported visibility of 10.8% corresponded to a true value of 1.9%. Nine points of illusion, because nobody bounded the noise.

Nadelio measures several times and reports the spread, not just the average. You get the score with its margin, and one honest word for how much to trust it.

The verdict word

Where you stand on Google versus in AI answers. Strong on one and invisible on the other is the most common, and most expensive, blind spot.

The lead that is real

When compared to a rival, Nadelio tells you whether a gap holds up or dissolves into noise. That one line prevents a lot of wasted budget.

Read together, these four answer the questions that actually get asked in a leadership meeting: are we visible where buyers now decide, is a competitor genuinely ahead, did our last move work, and can this figure survive scrutiny. ± Nadelio What we will not pretend
04

The honesty is the product.

Anyone can print a confident number. The harder, rarer thing is to tell you when the number cannot be trusted. That is the line Nadelio holds, on purpose.

We audit the right company

Before measuring, Nadelio reads your real website. If a name is ambiguous, we confirm it with you instead of confidently measuring the wrong business.

We never bound a single sample

With one measurement, we say so plainly rather than inventing a false precision. A margin you can trust is worth more than a number you cannot.

A wide margin is information, not a flaw

When the interval is wide, that is the honest signal that the model is unstable on your brand. Knowing that is something you can act on.

The models will keep changing. New assistants will arrive. That is exactly why a bounded, repeatable measurement matters more than a one time snapshot: it is the one reading that stays meaningful as the ground moves under it.
